Question 6

Q). Name and describe 4 different kinds of positioning in CSS.

Quiz Notes for Question 6:

Used class notes for the listing of static, for which I initally had float right, and relative, for which I initially had float left.

A). Four kinds of positioning in CSS are: Absolute, Fixed, Relative and Static.

I am listed 1st in the HTML document. My position is typically based on my nearest positioned ancestor and if the relationship doesn't exist, my position is relative to the position of the body of the document. When I assume my absolute position I move along with the scrolling of the page.
I am listed 2nd in the HTML document. Unlike my counterparts, I remain fixed or in place when scrolling and my position is relative to the browser window. Use the top, bottom, left or right properties to position me.
I'm in a static position and is listed 3rd in the HTML document. This is the default position of all elements and as my name implies, I remain in place or within the normal flow of the page.
My position is relative and I am listed 4th in the HTML document. What that means is that though I physically move out of position on the page, the space that I left is held and no other element takes it's place. When I am relatively position myself, it's based on my original location or my space in the line. Set my top and left margins in order to move me around.
