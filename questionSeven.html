    What is floating?

    Floating is the movement of an element or elements out of the normal flow of the document. Elements can be floated to the left or right of a page. Floating an element to the left allows anything that follows it to move to the right of it while floating an element to the right moves other elements following it, to the left, unless otherwise directed. CSS float is typically used to wrap text around images.
